Speakers

Alex Komoroske

Group Product Manager, Google

Alex is the lead product manager on Chrome's web platform team. Although he's been a fan of the web ever since he wrote his first web app, his appreciation deepened considerably when he investigated open ecosystems for his undergraduate thesis.

Twitter

Alex Russell

Software Engineer, Google

Alex Russell is a Software Engineer on Chrome team where he designs new features for the Web Platform and leads Chrome’s Standards work. He’s a member of ECMA TC39 , the committee standardizing JavaScript, and is an elected member of the W3C’s Technical Architecture Group where he works to improve the state of API design for the web. His recent projects include Web Components, ES6 features including Classes and Promises, and Service Workers. Previously he helped build Chrome Frame and led the Dojo Toolkit project. Alex plays for Team Web.

Twitter

Ali Alabbas

Program Manager, Microsoft

Ali is a Program Manager on the Microsoft Edge team. He manages the end-to-end scenarios involved with service workers in Edge. He is also a member of the W3C web platform working group.

Andreas Bovens

Opera for Android Product Manager & Dev Relations Lead

Andreas works for Opera Software, where he is product manager of Opera for Android and Dev Relations team lead. Over the last year, he’s been working on getting Opera to support the technology stack powering progressive web apps — successfully so! —, and he’s been talking about them at various developer events. Previously, he was PM for browser extensions, web evangelist, and product tester at Opera.

Andrew Mori

Director of Technology, Konga Online Shopping Ltd

Andrew Mori - a Physicist by academic history and a Computer Scientist by experience - is Directory of Technology at Konga, a leading e-commerce website in Nigeria. He and his team built the first Progressive Web App at a major ecommerce website in Africa. In his spare time Andrew is interested in all things science, especially space exploration and the concept of establishing non-terran human establishments.

Twitter

Ben Kelly

Platform Engineer, Mozilla

Ben Kelly is a software engineer on Mozilla's platform team. His main focus has been helping to implement and support service workers in Firefox.

Twitter

Chris Wilson

Developer Advocate, Google

Chris Wilson is a Developer Advocate for the Open Web platform. He started working on web browsers in 1993 when he co-authored the original Windows version of NCSA Mosaic, worked on Internet Explorer for fifteen years at Microsoft, and joined Google in 2010. He co-chairs the Web Incubation Community Group, is on the W3C Advisory Board, and just generally tries to make the web great.

Twitter

Daniel Applequist

Samsung Internet Ambassador; Co-Chair of W3C TAG

Daniel Appelquist is an open web advocate and Internet Ambassador at Samsung, working with the Samsung Browser team on developer advocacy and product strategy. He has over 10 years experience in web standards, most recently as co-chair of the W3C Technical Architecture Group. He also helped to start the W3C Mobile Web Initiative and he is the co-founder of Mobile Monday London, and the Over the Air mobile hack day series.

Twitter

Emily Schechter

Product Manager, Google

Emily Schechter is a product manager on the Chrome security team. She focuses on creating user-friendly, effective Chrome security, and on promoting HTTPS adoption across the web. Emily previously worked to protect Google and web users from malware, phishing, and malware-in-ads on Google's Safe Browsing and anti-malvertising teams. Emily has bachelors degrees in computer engineering and economics from Dartmouth College.

Eric Bidelman

Developer Programs Engineer at Google

Eric is a long time member of the Web DevRel team, based out of Mountain View, CA. Lately, he's been perfecting his digitial jedi skills by working closely with the Chrome and Polymer teams. He's the author of “Using the HTML5 Filesystem API” and has led frontend projects like the Google I/O web app, Google's Santa tracker, chromestatus.com, and html5rocks.com. Prior to Google, Eric worked as a software engineer at the University of Michigan where he designed web applications and APIs for the university’s 19 libraries.
